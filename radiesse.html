Radiesse FAQs

What is the difference between Radiesse and HA Fillers?

Hyaluronic Acid (HA) fillers work by instantly plumping and hydrating a specific fold or hollow. Radiesse (Calcium Hydroxylapatite) is a bio-stimulator; it works by triggering your body's own cells to produce new collagen and elastin, creating a long-term structural tightening effect.

What does "Hyper-Dilute" mean?

When used conventionally, Radiesse provides rigid volume. When we "hyper-dilute" it with saline and lidocaine, it becomes a thin wash. This allows us to spread the collagen-stimulating particles over a wide area (like the neck or cheeks) to tighten crepey skin without adding any puffiness or volume.

How long does it take to see results?

You may see a subtle initial improvement from the carrier gel, but true results take patience. Significant collagen production usually begins around 4 weeks and peaks at 3 months.

How many sessions are required?

Depending on the degree of skin laxity, most patients require 1 to 3 sessions spaced roughly 4 to 6 weeks apart to build the desired foundational matrix, followed by annual maintenance.
