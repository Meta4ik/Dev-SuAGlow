  • What Is Radiesse®?

Radiesse® is a calcium hydroxyapatite, or CaHA, biostimulator injectable designed to provide immediate structural support while also encouraging your skin to build collagen over time.

In simple terms:

Radiesse® gives structure first, then asks your skin to do some of the long-term work.

It is commonly used to improve the appearance of facial volume loss, contour definition, skin laxity, and age-related structural changes. Unlike traditional hyaluronic acid fillers, Radiesse® is often selected when the goal is firmer support, better definition, and collagen-stimulating improvement.

  • Is Radiesse® a Filler or a Biostimulator?

Radiesse® is often described as both a filler and a biostimulator.

Here is the difference:

Traditional filler usually focuses on replacing volume. Radiesse® can provide immediate contour support, but it also works as a collagen-stimulating treatment because its CaHA microspheres help support new collagen production over time.

That makes Radiesse® especially helpful for patients who want:

  • Sharper facial definition
  • Better lower-face support
  • Improved contour
  • Firmer skin appearance
  • More structural lift
  • A less “filled” look

Radiesse® is not about overcorrection. It is about strategic reinforcement.

  • Radiesse® vs Sculptra®: Which Biostimulator Is Better?

Radiesse® and Sculptra® are both biostimulatory treatments, but they behave differently.

Radiesse® is often chosen when the goal includes more immediate contour support, definition, and structural reinforcement. Sculptra® is often chosen when the goal is gradual, global collagen stimulation and soft volume restoration over a series of treatments.

A simple way to think about it:

  • Radiesse®: structure, contour, definition
  • Sculptra®: gradual collagen, soft fullness, global rejuvenation

Some patients benefit from one. Some benefit from both at different stages. The right answer depends on your anatomy, skin quality, age-related changes, and aesthetic goals.

  • Frequently Asked Questions About Radiesse®

Is Radiesse® good for jawline contouring?

Yes, Radiesse® may be a strong option for jawline contouring in appropriate candidates because it provides structural support and definition. It is especially useful when the goal is a cleaner lower-face contour rather than soft volume alone.

Is Radiesse® the same as filler?

Radiesse® is often categorized as a dermal filler, but it is also a biostimulator. It provides immediate support while also stimulating collagen over time.

Is Radiesse® better than Sculptra®?

Neither is universally better. Radiesse® is often selected for structure and contour, while Sculptra® is often selected for gradual, global collagen support. The right treatment depends on your anatomy and goals.

Can Radiesse® be used for the neck?

In some cases, diluted or hyperdiluted Radiesse® may be used to support skin firmness in areas such as the neck. A consultation is required to determine candidacy.

Does Radiesse® stimulate collagen?

Yes. Radiesse® contains calcium hydroxylapatite microspheres that support collagen stimulation over time.

How quickly will I see results?

Some improvement may be visible soon after treatment due to the immediate structural effect. Collagen-related improvements develop gradually over time.

How long does Radiesse® last?

Many patients experience results that may last a year or longer, depending on treatment area, product amount, metabolism, and individual response.

Is Radiesse® reversible?

Radiesse® is not dissolved with hyaluronidase like hyaluronic acid fillers. This is why proper patient selection, anatomy assessment, and conservative technique are important.

Is Radiesse® good for under eyes or lips?

Radiesse® is generally not used in delicate areas such as the lips or under-eyes. Other treatments may be better suited for those areas.

Who should avoid Radiesse®?

Patients with certain allergies, active infection, pregnancy, breastfeeding, unrealistic expectations, or specific medical conditions may not be appropriate candidates. Your provider will review your history before treatment.
